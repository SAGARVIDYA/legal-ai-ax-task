The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 protects consumers against unfair trade practices, defective goods, and deficient services. The Act provides a mechanism for consumers to file complaints and seek compensation.

Consumers have rights including the right to safety, the right to be informed, the right to choose, the right to be heard, and the right to seek redressal.

Consumer disputes can be filed before District, State, and National Consumer Commissions depending on the value of the claim. Businesses found guilty of unfair practices may face penalties and compensation orders.

The Act also addresses misleading advertisements and protects consumers engaged in online shopping and e-commerce transactions.
