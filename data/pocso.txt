The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012 is a law enacted to protect children below 18 years from sexual offences. The Act covers sexual assault, sexual harassment, and pornography involving children.

The law provides child-friendly procedures for reporting, recording evidence, investigation, and trial. Any person who becomes aware of an offence against a child can report it to the authorities.

The Act provides strict punishments for offenders, including imprisonment and fines. The objective is to ensure the safety, dignity, and protection of children.
